Understanding CGPA

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is a measure of a student's overall academic performance across multiple semesters or years. Unlike GPA, which is calculated for a single semester, CGPA reflects your cumulative performance.

Hereâ€™s how CGPA works:

  • Calculation: CGPA is calculated by averaging the GPA of all semesters.
  • Grading System: Each grade corresponds to a specific grade point (e.g., A = 4.0, B = 3.0).
  • Importance: CGPA is often used by employers and universities to assess a student's academic consistency.

Understanding your CGPA can help you set academic goals and track your progress over time.
